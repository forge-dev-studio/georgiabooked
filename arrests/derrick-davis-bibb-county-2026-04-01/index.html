Terroristic threats and acts is classified as a violent felony under Georgia law. Convictions on such charges carry significant statutory penalties, typically including substantial prison sentences and fines. The offense addresses communications or actions intended to intimidate or coerce the public or government, or which recklessly cause public evacuation or emergency response. Violent felony designations carry enhanced sentencing considerations under Georgia's criminal code and may affect eligibility for certain pretrial release options.
