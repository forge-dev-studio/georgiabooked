Michael Domingo

Booked Monday, April 13, 2026

Presumption of innocence. This is a public-record booking report. Michael Domingo has been charged but not convicted. All charges are allegations and the accused is presumed innocent until proven guilty in a court of law.

Michael Domingo was booked into the Whitfield County jail on April 13, 2026, and was charged with driving under the influence of alcohol, driving too fast for conditions, and failure to maintain lane. The charges stem from a traffic incident in which he is alleged to have operated a motor vehicle while impaired by alcohol and committed related traffic violations.

DUI charges in Georgia are classified as serious misdemeanors when they involve a first or subsequent offense without aggravating circumstances such as accidents, injuries, or extremely high blood alcohol content. Georgia law imposes mandatory minimum penalties for DUI convictions, including fines, license suspension, and potential jail time, with consequences escalating for repeat offenses. The accompanying traffic violations for unsafe speed and lane maintenance are commonly cited in conjunction with impaired driving incidents and reflect the broader scope of dangerous driving conduct.
