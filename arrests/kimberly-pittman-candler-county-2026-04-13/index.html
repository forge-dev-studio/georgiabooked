Kimberly Pittman

Presumption of innocence. This is a public-record booking report. Kimberly Pittman has been charged but not convicted. All charges are allegations and the accused is presumed innocent until proven guilty in a court of law.

Kimberly Pittman was booked into the Candler County jail on April 13, 2026. She was charged with obedience to traffic control devices required, knowingly driving a motor vehicle on suspended, canceled, or revoked registration, insurance requirements and proof of insurance, and possession of marijuana less than one ounce.

These charges constitute serious misdemeanors under Georgia law. Violations related to traffic control devices and driving with suspended or revoked registration are typically prosecuted as misdemeanors with potential penalties including fines and possible jail time. Possession of marijuana in quantities less than one ounce is classified as a misdemeanor in Georgia, carrying potential sentences of up to 12 months in jail and fines. Insurance-related violations are also handled as misdemeanor offenses. Defendants charged with serious misdemeanors in Georgia are entitled to legal representation and have the right to contest charges in court.
